Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML)

As I see
The rise of machine learning and artificial intelligence has ushered in a new era of computing, one in which machines can learn and adapt to new situations without explicit programming. This has led to the development of some of the most exciting and revolutionary technologies in recent times, such as GPT, AlphaZero, and DeepBlue. These technologies have captured my imagination and of IT professionals around the world, and for good reason.

GPT, AlphaZero, and DeepBlue represent the pinnacle of machine learning and artificial intelligence. These technologies have been able to achieve feats that were once thought impossible, such as beating the world's best chess players and mastering complex games like Go.

For me, these technologies represent a new frontier in computing, one in which machines can learn and adapt to new situations on their own. They also represent an opportunity to solve some of the most complex problems in the world, from climate change to healthcare. As such, they are a magnet for me as professional who are looking to push the boundaries of what is possible with technology and make a real difference in the world.

Today, in a company where AI is not its core business, what is a true AI solution and what is not?

Let's make an example with one of the AI heavyweights of our days.
In theory, if AlphaZero were reprogrammed by a third party company to play the role of CEO, production manager or something similar and trained with business and labor laws etc.. and geared to maximize economic gains and productivity, could he potentially achieve superhuman performance in that area?

I have to say that sound pretty cool and maybe possible in few year with required infrastructure could be feasible but in this moment not at all, for many factor such as:
*AlphaZero algorithm is not publicly available.
*Even that the research papers and technical documentation detailing the algorithm and its implementation are freely available. However, implementing the AlphaZero algorithm requires a significant amount of expertise in machine learning, deep neural networks, and reinforcement learning.
*The computing resources required, are expensive and not accessible to everyone.
*In resume a solution like AlphaZero system requires significant expertise and resources, so "IT IS NOT A REAL AI SOLUTION", at least not for a non-AI company.

Now lets chek what can we do with other AI heavyweight of our day GPT-3.

Same scenario, if GPT-3 were reprogrammed by a third party company to play the role of CEO, production manager or something similar and trained with business and labor laws etc.. and geared to maximize economic gains and productivity, could he potentially achieve superhuman performance in that area?

My answer is "Yes but" GPT-3 is a proprietary language model developed by OpenAI and OpenAI has made GPT-3 available to developers and businesses through its AI platform, OpenAI Codex. Codex provides access to GPT-3's natural language processing capabilities, as well as other AI models and tools, through an API that can be integrated into various applications and systems.

A way to implementing AI & ML in our I4.0 projects

For this exercise, lets Started with the fictitious scenario but with the very real needed of minimize waste and reduce energy consumption in a fictitious electric motor factory.
In this project, we aim to minimize waste and reduce energy consumption in an electric motor factory by implementing a machine learning model. The main objective is to optimize the production process by predicting the energy consumption and waste generation for each unit and providing actionable insights to reduce them.
